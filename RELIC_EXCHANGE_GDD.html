01 — Concept

The Core Idea

You are a broker of sacred bones, teeth, and splinters — bidding against AI-driven zealots in a fictional underground relic exchange for the scattered remains of saints.

HOLY_OPS.EXE is a single-player real-time auction game. Lots spawn continuously on a live grid. Player and AI bidders compete in timed auctions. Winning a lot adds the relic to your collection. Collect every relic of a saint to “complete” them. Budget is fixed at ♦ 5,000 — no refills.

The visual language is retro terminal / Bloomberg terminal meets medieval reliquary. Everything is sharp-cornered monospace text on a deep navy-black background. Currency glows gold. Danger pulses red. The grid breathes — lots swell as they approach expiry, demanding attention.

The Flip

Traditional auction games give you unlimited money and a time limit. HOLY_OPS inverts this: unlimited time, finite money. The pressure isn’t the clock — it’s the budget. Every bid is permanent. Every overbid is currency you’ll never get back. The game ends not with a buzzer, but with the quiet realisation that you can no longer afford anything.

Win Condition

Complete as many saints as possible before running out of currency. There is no formal end state — the game continues as long as there are lots to bid on and currency to spend. The goal is strategic accumulation under scarcity.

1. Scan. 18 lots fill the market grid at launch. New lots spawn every 5 seconds, up to 30 active simultaneously. The BSP treemap layout gives more screen space to urgent and bid-on lots.

2. Select. Click any grid cell or ticker row. The bid panel populates with lot details: relic name, saint progress, current bid, countdown timer.

3. Bid. Quick-bid buttons (+10, +50, +100) or manual input. Your bid must exceed the current highest. Currency is not deducted until you win.

4. Compete. AI bidders evaluate every 3 seconds. Their aggression escalates as lots near expiry. If outbid, the grid cell pulses red.

5. Resolve. When the timer hits zero: if your bid ≥ current bid, you win. The relic joins your collection. If a saint is completed, a full-screen celebration overlay fires.

6. Heresy. The terminal feed periodically reports heresy events from fictional sects. During these 4-second tribunal delays, AI bidders are frozen — a strategic window for unopposed bidding.

05 — Mechanics

Auction System

Lot Creation

A random saint is chosen, then a random uncollected relic from that saint. The lot enters the market with a starting bid between ♦ 15–150 and a duration of 45–150 seconds. Duplicate prevention: active lots cannot contain the same relic ID (up to 10 attempts before giving up).

Player Bidding

Validation requires: lot must exist and be active, amount must exceed currentBid, amount must not exceed currency. On success, the lot’s weight increases by 0.3 (capped at 3.0), keeping bid-on lots prominent in the grid. Currency is not deducted until the lot is won.

Design Decision

Deferred deduction creates a crucial tension: your “available” currency may be committed across several active bids. You might win them all — and go broke. The player must mentally track their exposure.

Lot Resolution

When timeRemaining ≤ 0:

Win: Player’s bid ≥ current bid → status = 'won', gold flash, relic added to saint’s collection, currency deducted. If saint now complete → completion overlay for 2.5 seconds.

Loss: Player bid but was outbid → status = 'lost', red flash, fades out after 3 seconds.

Closed: Player never bid → status = 'closed', removed silently.

All resolved lots are garbage-collected after 2 seconds (timeRemaining < -2).

06 — Rivals

AI Bidding

AI bidders evaluate every 3 seconds. For each active lot, a probability check determines whether an AI bid is placed. Aggression escalates with urgency:

Time RemainingBid ProbabilityBehaviour
< 10 seconds20%Aggressive sniping
< 30 seconds8%Active bidding
≥ 30 seconds3%Occasional interest

Bid increment: Random between 3 and max(8, currentBid × 8%). Small increments that accumulate — AI bidders are patient, not explosive.

Outbid signal: When AI outbids the player, the grid cell receives a red 'outbid' flash lasting 1.5 seconds. The cell’s border pulses red continuously until the player rebids or the lot expires.

Critical: AI bidders are completely paused during heresy slowdown events. This is the core strategic mechanic.

07 — Heresy

The Heresy System

The terminal feed displays a mix of system messages and heresy reports from five fictional heterodox sects. Heresy events create a strategic freeze window where the player can bid unopposed.

The Five Sects

Flagellants Cathari Gnostic Order Bogomils Waldensians

Message Generation

Every 2.5 seconds, a new message is generated: 35% chance heresy report (random sect + random template), 65% chance system message (benign status update). Messages are kept in a rolling buffer: max 80, trimmed to last 60 on overflow.

Heresy Templates

Tribunal alerts, intercepts, breach reports, sweeps, and edicts — all procedurally filled with a random sect name. System messages are terminal flavour text: “Market tick synchronized”, “Canonical verification: PASS”, etc.

The Slowdown

When a heresy report fires:

Tribunal Delay — 4 Seconds

The game tick alternates skipping (effectively half-speed timers). AI bidders are completely paused. The title bar shows █ TRIBUNAL DELAY █. The terminal header shows █ HERESY DETECTED — MARKET DELAY █.

Strategic implication: The player can bid during this window without AI competition. Skilled players learn to watch for heresy reports and bid aggressively during tribunal delays.

08 — Economy

Currency & Strategy

Strategic Notes

1. Budget is everything. Saints with 12+ relics require 12+ winning bids. At average bid costs of ♦ 100–300, a single saint can cost ♦ 1,200–3,600.

2. Exploit heresy freezes. When the tribunal delay hits, AI stops bidding for 4 seconds. Place bids during this window.

3. Watch the ticker, not the grid. The LotTicker’s “YOUR BIDS” section is the fastest way to find lots you’re losing.

4. Small saints are easier. Teresa (8 relics) and Agatha (9 relics) require fewer wins than Francis (13 relics).

5. Don’t overbid early. AI bid increments are small (3–8% of current bid). Match their pace, don’t jump ahead.

6. Closing lots get expensive. AI bid chance jumps to 20% in the final 10 seconds. Bid early or bid big.

10 — The Grid

BSP Market Grid

The market grid uses Binary Space Partitioning to dynamically size auction cells based on their weight. Higher-weight lots get more screen real estate.

Algorithm

1. Sort lots by weight (descending). 2. Choose split axis: horizontal if width ≥ height, vertical otherwise. 3. Accumulate weights from the top until reaching 40% of total weight → split point. 4. Split ratio clamped to 25%–75%. 5. Recurse on both halves. 6. Base case: single lot → return leaf node with 1px gap on all sides.

Minimum cell dimensions: 120×90 pixels.

Weight Dynamics

Weights range from ~0.8 to 3.0. The formula:

weight = 0.8 + (1 - timeRatio) × 0.8
       + min(bidCount × 0.1, 0.8)  // activity boost
       + (yourBid ? 1.0 : 0)         // your lots stay large
       + (timeRemaining < 15 ? 0.5 : 0) // urgency boost

This means lots you’ve bid on stay large and visible, and lots about to expire grow to signal urgency.

11 — Components

UI Components

LotTicker (Left Sidebar)

Two sections: YOUR BIDS (pinned at top) shows only lots where yourBid !== null, sorted by time remaining, with WIN/OUTBID status labels. ALL LOTS (scrollable below) shows every active lot. Clicking any row selects it.

BidPanel (Right Sidebar)

When a lot is selected: Lot ID, relic name, saint progress, current bid, timer, status indicator (WINNING / OUTBID). Quick bid buttons: +10, +50, +100. Manual bid input. Confirmation toast: “BID PLACED ♦ {amount}” for 2 seconds. ESC to close.

TerminalFeed

Scrolling heresy and system message log. Each message colour-coded by sect. Header shows █ HERESY DETECTED — MARKET DELAY █ during slowdowns.

Collection

Bottom of right sidebar (160px height). Won relics grouped by saint: saint name with progress fraction, 28×28 relic images with golden drop-shadow, hover tooltip showing relic name.

SaintTracker (Top Bar)

Currency display (♦ with gold colouring) + horizontal row of saints with progress bars showing collected/total as filled segments.

SaintCompleteOverlay

Full-screen celebration when a saint is completed. Semi-transparent dark backdrop, saint name in large Pirata One text, saint-reveal animation (2s scale + fade), auto-dismisses after 2.5 seconds.

15 — Audio

Audio Design

Currently a single audio effect: the wololo monk easter egg.

Signal Chain

AudioBuffer (wololo.mp3)
→
WaveShaper (soft clip)
→
BandpassFilter (800Hz, Q=2)
→
Gain (0.08)
→
Destination

Distortion curve: soft clipping via π+50 × x / (π + 50|x|). Bandpass filter creates a lo-fi, muffled effect. Very quiet (gain 0.08) to avoid startling the player. Fails silently if audio context is unavailable.

Key Decisions

No external state management. Game state is a single useState<GameState> in Index.tsx, updated via pure functions from engine.ts. This keeps the game loop simple and predictable.

Memoised components. GridCell, LotTicker, SaintTracker, TerminalFeed, Collection are all wrapped in React.memo to prevent unnecessary re-renders during the 1-second tick cycle.

Pure engine functions. All game logic (tick, placeBid, aiBids, spawnLot) are pure functions that take state and return new state — no side effects.

CSS-only animations. All visual effects use CSS keyframes rather than JS animation libraries.

18 — Performance

Performance Notes

With 30 lots rendering at a 1-second tick rate, performance is managed through:

React.memo on all child components. GridCell, LotTicker, SaintTracker, TerminalFeed, and Collection only re-render when their props actually change.

Stable references. useCallback for all event handlers. useMemo-style patterns for derived data (lot maps, saint maps).

BSP recalculation. Runs every tick but only on the active lot subset. The algorithm is O(n log n) and handles 30 items in <1ms.

Garbage collection. Resolved lots are cleaned up after 2 seconds to prevent memory growth.

Relic images. 31 golden reliquary images assigned via consistent hashing (hashCode(relicId) % RELIC_IMAGES.length) — same relic always gets the same image across sessions.
